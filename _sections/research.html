I work on a few projects involving the application and formalization of new symbolic machine learning algorithms. The main research line revolves around the idea of equipping standard decision trees with the ability of performing spatial or temporal reasoning, so that they can natively handle time series, images, videos and, more generally, data that extends along temporal and/or spatial dimensions. This is done by adopting logical formalisms that are able to express entity-relatio reasoning, for example, predicate logic or modal logic. The team I am working with is a mixture of logicians and machine learning people interested in interpretability; we have been following this lead for three years, achieving remarkable results for some tasks of time series classification and image classification.

My work is supervised by Prof. Guido Sciavicco.
