--- title: Why ¿AI? icon: fa-search order: 4 ---

Doing stuff can be entertaining; but I generally find automating stuff more entertaining. First, automating something forces you to understand it, which is good stimuli for the mind. Furthermore, if you manage to automate some non-entertaining task, you can, then, choose to never do that thing again, and spend your time doing something else (e.g., playing with your dog).
Some non-trivial tasks are difficult to automate. For example, humans can detect something is weird in an image effortlessly, while teaching a computer to "distinguish weird from not weird" (whatever that means) is considerably harder. This typically happens when no perfect low-level procedure exists for solving the task, or because the required thought process is complex and not fully understood, even by humans. In these cases forms of non-trivial reasoning must be sourced, and Machine Learning, today's primary enabler of Artificial Intelligence (AI), seeks the solution in data.
Data has the answers to many hard questions and, these days, when you have good data, you just have to word your questions in the right way.
